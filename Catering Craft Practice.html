Catering Craft Practice-ESSAY-ANSWERS

(1a)


(i) Eyes: Fresh fish exhibit bright, clear, and convex eyes, be indicative of their vitality and quality.

(ii) Gills: The gills of fresh fish present a vibrant red or pink hue, devoid of any traces of slime or mucus. Conversely, pallid or grayish gills, or those encased in a thick layer of mucus, signify a lack of freshness.

(iii) Flesh: Fresh fish possess firm and lustrous flesh that displays resilience when gently pressed. The flesh should promptly spring back, indicative of its freshness.

(iv) Smell: The olfactory examination of fresh fish reveals a mild and subtly sweet aroma, evocative of the sea. Any discernible unpleasant odors may suggest a decline in freshness

(1b)



(i) Chunks: These are small, uniform pieces of fish ideal for various culinary preparations, such as incorporation into soups or stews.

(ii) Loins: The loin sections represent the premium cuts extracted from the back of the fish, suitable for grilling or pan-frying due to their robust texture.

(iii) Steaks: Thick slices of fish cut crosswise, rendering them suitable for grilling or broiling, as they maintain their structural integrity during cooking.

(iv) Fillets: These boneless and skinless portions of fish are versatile and well-suited for baking, sautéing, or pan-frying, offering a delicate and tender texture.
